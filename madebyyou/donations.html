Your donations and how they are used

Why do you need donations?

Simple: XKit is free of charge, always has been, always will be. I never do anything that can remotely make me money, for example: I've been contacted by four corporations around the world who specializes in targeted advertising, asking me to give them anonymous XKit user data for a monthly fee. Even though they assured me they are completely anonymous, I've rejected, because your data, your usage patterns and habits belong to you: they should not be processed, parsed and used to make money. Since I reject offers like this, I not only not make money off XKit, I actually, some months, lose money.
 

What else?

I spend at least ~2 hours daily (I've been spending over 9 hours daily in order to prepare XKit 7) to respond to asks, update extensions, and make sure every server and extension is up and running. This is a time consuming hobby.
 

How is my money used?

Approximately, since I'm always upgrading or downgrading servers depending on usage. (Upgrading these days, a lot, though)

Most of the spending goes to XIM servers, followed by XKit Servers (used to push News, updates, and install XKit itself.)

The rest goes to domain names such as XKit.info and studioxenix.com and Voxous.com (used by XIM), this represents a really small percentage since they are yearly. (around $80/year)

Coke1 is bought only when there is extra money left by the end of the month, to keep me awake.
[1] Coca-Cola, that is. They don't sell Vanilla Coke where I live btw, that makes me sad.
 

What did the donations do so far for XKit?

First, it encouraged me to spend so much time on XKit 7, which resulted in one of the most stable and fastest XKit, ever. Secondly, with that money, I was able to buy, then upgrade, the XIM servers, which you can now use with much, much less downtime. (When XIM was first released, it would be down for ~8-9 hours every day, it is less than 20 minutes every 2 weeks now.)
