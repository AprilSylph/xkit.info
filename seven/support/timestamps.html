Timestamps format

You can customize your timestamps using the syntax below. XKit uses moment.js, so it uses it's syntax.
Token
Description
Example
Month
M
Month number
6
Mo
Month number
6th
MM
Month number with leading zeros
06
MMM
Short month name
Dec
MMMM
Long month name
December
Day
D
Day of month
1
Do
Day of month
1st
DD
Day of month with leading zeros
01
Day of Week
d
Day of week as a number
0, 1, 2, .... 6
ddd
Day of week as short text
Sun, Mon, .. Fri, Sat
dddd
Day of week as long text
Sunday, Monday, Friday, Saturday
Year
YY
Short Year
84, 94, 04
YYYY
Long Year
1984, 1994, 2004
Time
A
AM/PM, uppercase
AM
a
AM/PM, lowercase
am
h
Hour (12-hour)
1, 2, ... 11, 12
hh
Hour with leading zeros (12-hour)
01, 02, ... 11, 12
H
Hour (24-hour)
0, 1, ... 22, 23
HH
Hour with leading zeros (24-hour)
00, 01 ... 22, 23
m
Minute
0, 1, ... 58, 59
mm
Minute with leading zeros
00, 01, ... 58, 59
s
Second
0, 1, ... 58, 59
ss
Second with leading zeros
00, 01, ... 58, 59
Miscellaneous
[ text ]
Escape text, used to add text to timestamps
See example below
Z
Timezone
-07:00
X
Unix Timestamp
 

Some examples

What to type
Example
MMMM Do YYYY, h:mm:ss a
June 16th 2013, 1:19:00 pm
dddd, h:mm:ss A
Sunday, 1:19:00 PM
[on the day] MMM DD [around] hh:mma
on the day Jun 16 around 01:19pm
 

Where to type all these stuff?

Click on the X icon on your dashboard to bring up the XKit Control Panel.
From there, click on Timestamps, and type them into the "Timestamp Format" box.
